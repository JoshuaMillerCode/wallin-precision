# Firearms Training Content

## Tone
Use a calm, professional, safety-first tone.

Known project context:
- business name: `Wallin Precision`
- current public tagline: `Safe. Confident. Capable.`
- current public positioning: beginner-friendly and advanced firearms training, plus concealed carry training
- current public location: `Unionville, NC`
- concealed carry training is the primary offer
- the site should support both online booking and inquiry-first contact

Prefer:
- clear
- responsible
- instructional
- welcoming to beginners
- credibility-focused

Avoid:
- macho framing
- sensational language
- fear-based sales tactics
- claims that overpromise legal outcomes or tactical performance

## Messaging Priorities
Lead with:
1. safety
2. training quality
3. clarity about who the course is for
4. logistics and expectations
5. trust and transparency

For this project, keep copy welcoming to both:
- first-time students looking for safe, structured instruction
- experienced shooters seeking more advanced training

But in page hierarchy and CTA language:
- let concealed carry lead when presenting primary offerings
- keep other training options visible without competing with the main offer

## Content Rules
- Be precise about what a class covers.
- Separate legal education from legal advice.
- Do not imply guaranteed permits, outcomes, or proficiency.
- Make prerequisites, required gear, costs, and age restrictions explicit.
- When location matters, be specific about city, range, or service area.
- When writing about concealed carry classes, describe laws and requirements carefully and avoid promising permit approval.
- If details came only from the current Facebook page, preserve them as draft facts until the user confirms they should appear on the site.

## Common Website Sections
For course pages, include:
- course overview
- who it is for
- what students will learn
- what to bring
- duration and schedule
- pricing
- booking CTA
- contact/help CTA
- FAQ

For trust sections, include only verifiable items such as:
- instructor background
- certifications
- years teaching
- reviews
- facility information

## Copy Patterns
Prefer phrases like:
- `Learn safe firearm handling from an experienced instructor.`
- `Beginner-friendly instruction with clear, structured guidance.`
- `Understand course expectations, required gear, and next steps before booking.`
- `Safe. Confident. Capable.`
- `Training built for both new and experienced shooters.`
- `Contact us by phone or email if you need help choosing the right class.`
- `Reserve your spot online or contact us if you have questions first.`

Avoid phrases like:
- `Become unstoppable`
- `Guaranteed permit approval`
- `Master firearms overnight`

## Compliance Guardrails
When reviewing copy, flag:
- implied guarantees
- ambiguous legal claims
- missing disclaimers where needed
- unclear prerequisites
- language that could reduce trust with cautious first-time students

## Output Pattern
When asked to draft or revise copy, provide:
1. target audience
2. section copy
3. trust/compliance notes
4. CTA options
